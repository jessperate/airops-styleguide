04

Voice & Copy

Direct
Say what you mean. No fluff, no filler. Lead with the value.
Sharp
Short sentences. Strong verbs. Active voice always.
Expert
We know AEO. Use the right terminology confidently.
Human
Warm but never corporate. We're people talking to people.
Do
"See exactly where your content gets cited by AI."
Specific, active, leads with the user benefit.
"AirOps tracks AI citations so you can optimize what actually matters."
Clear cause and effect. No jargon padding.
"You're losing AI visibility. Here's why, and how to fix it."
Direct, slightly bold, gives a reason to keep reading.
Don't
"Leveraging next-generation AI-powered solutions to supercharge your content strategy."
Avoid buzzword stacking. Say what the product actually does.
"Our comprehensive, robust platform enables teams to synergize their content workflows."
No "synergize," "robust," "comprehensive," or "seamless."
"We are excited to announce our groundbreaking new feature..."
Never start with "We are excited to announce." Just announce it.
Punctuation & formatting rules
/* AirOps Copy Rules */ Use en dash () for ranges and pauses: "1020 citations" Never use em dash () Sentence case for headlines: "AEO is the new SEO" Not title case: "AEO Is The New SEO" Oxford comma: "ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity" Product names always capitalized: AirOps, Page360, Citations360, Brand Kit, AEO CTA copy: short imperatives "Get a demo" "Start free" "See it live" "Book a call" Not: "Click here to learn more about our platform"
